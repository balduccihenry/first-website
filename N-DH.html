Non-Diatonic Harmonies - Henry Balducci

Similar to diatonic, the harmony can also be traced back to a singular scale but these harmonies can have notes outside of the chord structure, adding tension.

  • These harmonies can add color to songs making them much more complex.

  • Common examples include chromatic chords, borrowed chords from other keys, and modal interchange.

  • Non-diatonic harmonies are frequently used in jazz, blues, and contemporary music to create unique harmonic textures.

  • They provide composers and musicians with greater freedom for experimentation and expression compared to strict diatonic harmonies.
